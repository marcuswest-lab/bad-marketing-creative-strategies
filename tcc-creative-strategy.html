1 · Offer Snapshot

WhatA done-for-you hiring system built exclusively for construction companies. TCC builds a branded hiring "engine" once, then runs the ads, funnels, sourcing, and initial screening year-round and hands the owner 3–5 pre-vetted finalists per role — "finalists only, every time." The owner just says yes or no. Roles covered: foremen, skilled trades, project managers, site superintendents, estimators, office staff, ops managers, CDL drivers.
Price / modelOne flat yearly fee — no per-hire recruiter commissions, no per-role charges. Positioned explicitly against staffing agencies ("recruiter commissions removed") and against the owner burning cash on job boards and bad hires. "95% done for you."
Core mechanismAn always-on, owned hiring machine (internally the "Growth Hiring Partner" / GHP): built once, reactivated whenever a new role opens with no rebuild. TCC pre-vets and screens internally, so the owner never sources, posts, or sifts résumés — they only interview finalists.
AudienceConstruction business owners / operators scaling their crews — the BUYER, not the tradesperson. Owners tired of ghosting candidates, no-shows, recruiter fees, and roles that stay open for months while bids get turned down. B2B lead-gen; broad US, later refined by trade (HVAC, electrical, plumbing, roofing, remodel, concrete, GC).
Landing pagethecontractorconsultants.com/meta-2 + Meta lead forms. Headline: "Stop Wasting Money on Recruiters and Bad Hires." Sub: "Flat fee. Construction only. 95% done for you." Primary CTA repeated ~6× as "FIX MY HIRING."
Proof assets5-star Google Reviews; named client wins ("helped us find two general managers within a week," "huge influx of highly qualified candidates" — Matthew Terry); "Finalists Only. Every Time."; "Goodbye No-Shows"; "Simply Say Yes or No"; dedicated account-manager team. Historical account: 300+ closed clients, ~$930K+ tracked spend, ~328 customers.
Formats live nowSpokesperson video (M/F, self-shot + studio), animated explainer (physics-fail / "This Is What Happens" hooks), AI talking-head, BlackText/BoldText "ugly" statics, native email screenshots (Gmail dark/light, Outlook — "The Owner Trap"), market-data statics (Job Cost Report / MarketSnapshot), testimonial video, meme/satire ("Recruiter Funeral," billboard).
What's workingVolume + conversions concentrate in Problem-Solution lead type and the Problem Aware / Most Aware stages. Proven concept families: MarketSnapshot / Job Cost Report (data-as-drama), "We're not recruiters" reframe, Recruiter Tax, The Owner Trap (email POV), Rejection & Revelation (per-trade), Never To This (burning-laptop promise), Two General Managers testimonial. Unaware and Product Aware are essentially empty spend — the biggest open lanes.

2 · Micro-Personas

All personas are the construction business OWNER (the buyer) — never the tradesperson being hired. This keeps every ad on the B2B side and out of Meta's Employment Special Ad Category (see §8).

A · "The Bleeding-Money Operator"

Primary desireStop hemorrhaging cash on recruiter fees, job-board spend, and hires that quit in a month.
Core fearThat hiring is a permanent tax he'll pay forever with nothing to show for it.
Core belief"Every recruiter and job board just wants another check — none of them actually solve it."
Unlocks: the Recruiter Tax / "we're not recruiters" reframe. Flat fee vs. per-hire commission, and a system he owns instead of renting. Data-as-drama (what a bad hire really costs) hits hardest here.

B · "The Turning-Down-Work Owner"

Primary desireSay yes to more bids and bigger projects instead of turning them down for lack of crew.
Core fearWatching competitors take the jobs he had to pass on because he couldn't staff them.
Core belief"I'd grow if I could just find the people — the work is there, the crew isn't."
Unlocks: the growth/opportunity-cost angle — "every week that role stays open is money walking to your competitor." Ties the open req directly to lost revenue, not HR pain.

C · "The Buried-In-Hiring Owner"

Primary desireGet his nights and weekends back — stop being the company's recruiter on top of running it.
Core fearThat hiring will keep eating the time he's supposed to spend on the business and his family.
Core belief"Sifting résumés and chasing no-shows is a full-time job I never signed up for."
Unlocks: "The Owner Trap" (email POV) + "done-for-you / you only say yes or no." The personal-life reclaim ("Wife Won't Kiss You" / back-pocket-hiring) framing lands here.

D · "The Burned-By-No-Shows Owner"

Primary desireInterview people who actually show up and can actually do the work.
Core fearAnother round of ghosting, no-call-no-shows, and "qualified" applicants who can't run a saw.
Core belief"Everybody who applies is either flaky or unqualified. The good ones aren't on the job boards."
Unlocks: "Finalists Only / Goodbye No-Shows" + pre-vetted screening. Rejection & Revelation, told per-trade (HVAC, electrical, plumbing, roofing) so it reads as written for his exact world.

E · "The 'Hiring-Is-Just-Hard' Owner"

Primary desireBelieve the labor shortage is just how it is and grind through it like everyone else.
Core fear(Latent) — hasn't connected slow growth and constant turnover to a fixable system problem.
Core belief"Nobody wants to work in the trades anymore. There's nothing to do but keep posting and hoping."
Unlocks: the Unaware / education angle — "it's not a labor shortage, it's a hiring-system problem." The single most under-worked lane in the account (see matrix).

5 · Week-by-Week Execution Playbook (Dummy-Proof)

This is the literal "what do I do this week" manual. Follow it in order. It assumes a media buyer + a creative person and the 2-Campaign Method below. B2B note: TCC sells a hiring service to owners, so consideration is longer and volume is lower than DTC — judge on cost per booked demo and cost per qualified (showed-up, is-a-contractor) demo, give ads a few days before calling it, and always retarget no-shows (see refill).

DayJobExactly what to do

THU — READCheck against Kill/Keep/ScaleOnce ads clear the spend gate (§5-B), score each on cost per booked demo / cost per qualified demo. Tag each ad KILL / KEEP / SCALE. Note which persona × stage cell and which format won, so next week leans that way.
FRI — CUT & PROMOTEKill losers, scale winnersTurn off KILLs. Move SCALE winners' post IDs into Campaign 2 (scaling) so social proof carries over. Weekly testing budget (rule of thumb): keep ~60–70% of budget in C2 (scaling winners) and ~30–40% in C1 (testing). Because a booked demo costs real money in B2B, give each new testing ad set enough to reach the spend gate — budget roughly 1–2× your target cost per booked demo per ad set before judging (e.g. plan for a couple hundred dollars of runway per new ad, not $20). If cash is tight, test fewer than 5 cells rather than starving all of them.
Exact 2-Campaign Method:
C1 — TESTING: every new ad goes here as its own new ad set (broad-ish audience, 1 angle each). This is where you discover winners. Never scale inside C1.
C2 — SCALING: only proven winners live here, added as their existing post ID (so likes/comments/shares follow the ad). Scale by raising budget ~15–20% every 2–3 days or by duplicating the winning ad set. Kill nothing here unless it decays below the KEEP line.
Ad naming convention (use every time): TCC_[Format]_[Persona]-[Stage]_[AngleName]_[Variant]_[MMDDYY]  →  e.g. TCC_Spokesperson_A-SolAware_WereNotRecruiters_v1_071926 or TCC_Static_D-Problem_GoodbyeNoShows_v2_071926. Consistent names make Thursday's read and the refill loop trivial.

D · How to Refill the Machine

  • Winners → move the post ID into C2, then brief 2–3 spins of each (new hook, new talent/trade, new format wrapper) so the proven angle keeps producing without fatiguing. This is how C2 stays fed.
  • Losers → don't delete the idea — send it to an Angle-Bank rebuild (§7): keep the angle, change the execution (different persona, stage, format, or emotional entry) and re-test in C1.
  • Fill the matrix over ~3 months: each week you clear ~5 more Persona × Stage cells. Prioritize the still-empty Unaware and Product Aware lanes until every cell has at least one tested asset — that's a fully-mapped account and a durable testing pipeline.
If you only do one thing this week: ship the 5 spokesperson concepts from §6 into C1 as 5 new ad sets, wait for the spend gate, then move any winner's post ID into C2 and retarget the no-shows. Everything else is optimization.

8 · Compliance & Brand-Voice Notes

Employment Special Ad Category — the key riskThese ads sell a hiring service to business owners (B2B), which is NOT an employment ad. But Meta's classifier looks at the copy: if an ad talks about job opportunities, wages, hours, or "we're hiring" for tradespeople directly, it can be flagged into the Employment Special Ad Category — which restricts audience targeting and can throttle/reject the ad. Rule: every ad addresses the OWNER as the buyer ("fill your roles," "hand off your hiring"), never the candidate ("apply now," "$X/hr," "now hiring electricians"). Avoid on-image "HIRING" / "APPLY" language and job-listing layouts. Keep the CTA on booking a demo, not applying for work.
No outcome guaranteesDon't promise a specific number of hires, a fill time, or a revenue result as a guarantee. Frame proof as illustrative and conditional ("one owner found two GMs in a week," "designed to deliver 3–5 finalists per role") — never "we guarantee you'll hire in X days."
Testimonials must be real / labeledUse genuine client quotes (Matthew Terry, "two general managers in a week," Google reviews). If a testimonial visual is a dramatization or uses a stock actor, label it on-image. Don't fabricate named results.
No unverifiable superlativesAvoid "the best," "#1," or "guaranteed" claims that can't be substantiated. "Construction only" and "flat fee" are factual and safe; keep hype grounded in the offer.
No personal-attribute targeting languageDon't imply Meta knows the viewer's business struggles ("we know your company is failing to hire"). Speak to the situation, not an assumed personal attribute.
Text coverage & legibilityKeep on-image text under ~20%; legible at 400px (mobile-first). Per-trade static swaps must stay readable.
Brand voiceBlue-collar-direct, confident, a peer who's been in the field — not corporate HR-speak. Talks the owner's language (bids, crews, foremen, no-shows). Plain-spoken, a little swagger, zero jargon. Kill AI-tells (leverage, unlock, seamless, supercharge, game-changer). Sound like a contractor talking to a contractor.
