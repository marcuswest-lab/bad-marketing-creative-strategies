The Nurse Coaches — Meta Creative Strategy

Free live training → NC-BC nurse-coach certification & coaching-business program (webinar funnel, Meta).

Primary KPI: ~$1,500 CPA (enrollment) at 2x ROAS  •  Secondary KPI: ~$30 cost per event registration at ~2% reg→sale  •  Win threshold: 3–5 enrollments or a registration cohort holding ≤$30 CPR before scaling a concept.

1 · Offer Snapshot

What: A free weekly live training ("FREE LIVE TRAINING — FOR NURSES ONLY") that introduces nurse coaching, then routes registered/qualified nurses into the board-certified (NC-BC) Nurse Coach certification + coaching-business program. Program price is not published on the LP; it is presented and closed on the call/enrollment step (high-ticket, application/enroll flow).

Funnel: Ad → LP registration (thenursecoaches.com/live-event) → live event/webinar → enroll/book. CTA on page: "Reserve Your Free Seat."

Audience: Licensed nurses — primarily active-license RNs (~78% of registrants), plus LPN/LVN (~9%). Gate out "not a nurse" and inactive-license traffic at the form.

Proof assets on hand

  • 4,600+ nurses already through the program (used in live statics)
  • 22+ filmed video testimonials (RN → nurse coach transformations)
  • NC-BC board-certification credential as the credibility spine
  • LP testimonials: Kayy O'Connell BSN RN NC-BC, Shanti Woodside, Caitlin Crawford

2 · Micro-Personas (psychographic)

Built from the registration-survey data (n≈14,200). Top stated desires: freedom/flexibility (63%), help patients truly heal (60%), holistic approach (60%), make a real difference (59%), work-life balance (58%). Top pains: exhausted/burnt out (47%), fed up with sick-care model (40%), don't feel valued (38%). Note: desire outweighs pain — lead with the future, not just the wound.

A · The Burnt-Out Bedside RN

Primary desire: To stop dreading shifts and feel like herself again — off the floor without leaving nursing.
Core fear: That she'll white-knuckle another decade, break down, and it'll have cost her health for nothing.
Core belief: "The system is broken and I'm powerless inside it."
Angle it unlocks: "You're not too weak for nursing — you're in the wrong version of it." Reframe burnout as a system problem, not a personal failing.

B · The Purpose-Driven Healer

Primary desire: To actually help people heal — root cause, lifestyle, connection — not manage symptoms and discharge.
Core fear: That she's become part of the assembly line she got into nursing to fix.
Core belief: "Real healing is holistic, and hospitals won't let me practice that way."
Angle it unlocks: Coaching = the holistic, root-cause nursing you always wanted to practice. Purpose over paycheck (money comes second).

C · The Freedom-Seeking Provider

Primary desire: Control her schedule and income — a flexible practice that fits her life, not the other way around.
Core fear: Trading a stable paycheck for something that doesn't replace it; being "irresponsible."
Core belief: "There's no way to earn well in nursing without giving up my whole life to shifts."
Angle it unlocks: A credible path to a flexible practice you run on your terms. Time-freedom + income framed realistically (results vary).

D · The Credential-Cautious Skeptic

Primary desire: A legitimate, board-recognized path — not another "coaching" gimmick.
Core fear: Wasting money on a program that isn't real or respected.
Core belief: "If it's not board-certified, it's not for serious nurses."
Angle it unlocks: NC-BC board certification + 4,600+ nurses + real testimonials. Answers "is this legit?" head-on.

E · The Life-Transition Nurse (new mom / near-retirement / injured)

Primary desire: Nursing that survives a life change — a new baby, a body that can't do 12s anymore, a phase-down before retirement.
Core fear: Being forced to choose between the career she trained for and the life she's living now.
Core belief: "Bedside nursing and my current life can't coexist."
Angle it unlocks: Life-stage hooks (the proven "Pregnancy angle") — nurse coaching keeps your license and identity working when the floor no longer fits.

4 · Format Rotation Plan

Which formats fit & why

  • Reels / short video (lead format): The Faceless "healthcare is broken" video is the proven account winner; testimonial videos are the credibility engine. Video carries story + proof, which this emotional, high-ticket transition needs.
  • Ugly / text-on-image (Apple Notes, Gmail, iMessage): Cheap to produce, native-feeling, proven registration drivers. Great for S1–S3 confessional and DM-style angles.
  • Lifestyle (Group Pic + Text): Warm/retargeting — "join 4,600+ nurses," community proof.
  • Meme: Calculated risk (~15%). Nurse-culture in-jokes ("charting at 2am," "another mandatory overtime") → shares → cheaper CPMs, new reach.

7 · Compliance & Brand-Voice Notes

Income / career-outcome claims (coaching vertical)

  • No guaranteed income or "you will make 6 figures." Frame as possible: "nurses are doing this," "results vary," "takes real work."
  • Any "6-figure" or earnings mention needs a visible "Results vary / not typical, no earnings guaranteed" disclaimer on the asset and LP.
  • Don't imply leaving your job or replacing your salary is easy or fast. Avoid "quit nursing" as a promise; use "off the floor without leaving nursing."

Health-adjacent framing

  • The buyer is the nurse, not a patient. Never imply the viewer has a medical condition. Burnout language targets the professional's career experience, which is fine.
  • Don't make patient-outcome medical claims ("cure," "heal disease"). Use "coach patients on lifestyle and root causes," "support healing," "holistic."

Credential accuracy

  • Only state board-certification (NC-BC) as the program pathway supports it. Don't overstate scope of practice or licensure.
  • Proof numbers ("4,600+ nurses") must stay current and defensible.

Meta policy hygiene

  • No "you"-shaming personal-attribute callouts ("Are YOU a failing nurse?"). Speak with the nurse, not at a deficiency.
  • Testimonials: keep them real, named, with credentials; add "individual results vary."

Brand voice

  • Warm, peer-to-peer, one-nurse-to-another. Sounds spoken, not written. Lead with the future they want; validate the pain without wallowing.
  • Kill AI-tells (unlock, leverage, seamless, supercharge). No staccato bursts. One idea per paragraph; isolate the strongest line.
