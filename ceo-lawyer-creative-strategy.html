CEO Lawyer — Meta Creative Strategy

"Find out how much your case is worth" — free 45-second case-value quiz for injury victims (Ali Awad, The CEO Lawyer, Atlanta).

Funnel: Meta lead-gen → quiz-style LP → phone/email capture → intake call → signed case  |  Primary KPI: Cost per Signed Case (blended CPA target ≈ $2,500; ≈ $2,000 on FB). Leading indicators: CPL, cost per 3+ (severity) lead, cost per Wanted lead.  |  Win threshold: 3–5 signed cases from one creative before it's a proven winner; a Tier-2 winner sustains $10k+ spend (e.g. GotACheck video hit $11k, Megan Collins Gmail static hit $16.4k).

1. Offer Snapshot

The offer. A free, 45-second quiz that estimates what an accident claim could be worth. Headline on LP: "Accident claims average $20,000–$250,000. What is your accident worth?" No cost to check; the firm works on contingency (you pay nothing unless they win).
Quiz mechanism (6 steps): accident type → when it happened → medical treatment? → injury severity (1–5) → who was at fault → do you have a lawyer yet? Then name / email / phone.
CTA: "See what your case is worth" / "Take the 45-second quiz" → Learn More (low-friction quiz, not a call booking).
Audience. Recent US accident victims (car, motorcycle, truck, slip-and-fall, work injury) in approved states, plus a large Spanish-speaking segment and a proven Arabic segment. Runs as a Special Ad Category–adjacent legal lead-gen account (broad, creative-led targeting).
Proof assets available: real client result ("We helped our client Jose get $675,000"); library of filmed testimonials with on-screen dramatization disclaimers (Allen, Sanchez, Nancy, Nichols); Ali-on-camera + creator-collab UGC (Devonte, Farzy); Spanish & Arabic testimonial masters.
Formats in use today: Gmail/Reddit/Twitter "screenshot" statics (top static winners), testimonial videos, Ali + creator collab check/settlement videos, Spanish & Arabic talking-head, Truck-accident vertical.
What the deals-won data actually rewards: the top signed-case drivers are (1) the Spanish Mr. Sanchez testimonial video (by far #1, 59+27 deals), (2) the Gmail/Reddit/Twitter "leaked email" statics under the "You Could Be Owed Thousands" headline, and (3) the Ali + creator "I got a check" collab videos. Every winner shares one spine: don't-settle-for-less / the insurance company is lowballing you.

2. Micro-Personas (psychographic)

P1 — The Lowballed Settler

Primary desire: to not get cheated out of money that's rightfully theirs after everything they went through.
Core fear: signing the insurance company's first offer and finding out later it was a fraction of what the case was worth.
Core belief: "The insurance company is not on my side — they're trained to pay me as little as possible."
Angle unlocked: "Before you accept that check, find out what your case is actually worth." (The single highest-converting spine in the account.)

P2 — The Overwhelmed Recoverer

Primary desire: peace of mind — to just heal while someone else handles the phone calls, bills and paperwork.
Core fear: medical bills piling up while they're too hurt and stressed to fight anyone.
Core belief: "This is too complicated and I don't have the energy for a legal battle right now."
Angle unlocked: "You focus on healing. We'll deal with the insurance company." (Testimonial + Ali reassurance.)

P3 — The Wrong-Lawyer Regretter

Primary desire: to have a lawyer who actually fights and communicates, not one who parked their case.
Core fear: being stuck with a lawyer who took the case and went quiet, leaving money on the table.
Core belief: "You can't switch lawyers mid-case" (false — a strong reframe angle).
Angle unlocked: "You can fire your lawyer and switch — even in the middle of your case — and it costs you nothing." (Ali "you don't buy a lawyer" collab.)

P4 — El Accidentado Hispano (Spanish-first)

Primary desire: justicia y respeto — un abogado que hable su idioma y no lo trate como un número.
Core fear: que por su estatus, su idioma o por no saber, le paguen mucho menos de lo que merece.
Core belief: "No importa mi situación migratoria — tengo derecho a una compensación justa."
Angle unlocked: testimonio real en español (Sr. Sánchez) — "recibí un cheque que no imaginaba." Highest-converting segment in the whole account.

P5 — The Big-Truck / Commercial-Crash Victim

Primary desire: to hold a big company accountable and get a settlement that matches how badly they were hurt.
Core fear: a corporate trucking insurer with an army of lawyers steamrolling them alone.
Core belief: "A crash with an 18-wheeler is a different, bigger fight than a fender-bender."
Angle unlocked: "Hit by a commercial truck or bus? These cases are worth far more — and far harder to win alone." (Trucking vertical winner.)

4. Format Rotation Plan

Which formats fit CEO Lawyer, and why:
  • Ugly / Screenshot Ads (LEAD FORMAT). The Gmail, Reddit and Twitter "leaked message" statics are the account's proven static workhorse (Megan Collins Gmail White = $16.4k winner; every top static rides them). Native, un-ad-like, high trust for a suspicious vertical. Always ship dark + light.
  • Testimonial Reels/Video. Real client stories with dramatization disclaimer — the single biggest signed-case driver (Spanish Sanchez). Highest intent-to-signed conversion.
  • Creator-collab UGC (partnership ads). Ali + Devonte / Farzy "I got a check" street-interview style. Injects a completely different signal set (creator handle, audience, style) — exactly the diversity Andromeda rewards. Run partnership ads inside the core campaign, not siloed.
  • Meme. Under-used. Insurance-lowball tension ("first offer" vs "what you were owed"; "we have full coverage" reality) is meme-native, drives shares, lowers CPMs.
  • AI Ads. Calculated-risk lane: AI check/settlement visuals or AI voiceover of a winning testimonial script as a 3rd-person "case explainer."

5. Week-by-Week Creative Production Playbook (Dummy-Proof)

If you run creative for this account and read nothing else, run this. It's a pure production cadence: pick the week's format, choose five Persona × Stage cells, write copy, build five creatives, package each finished concept for hand-off, then review what's resonating and reload. All KPI targets stay in the top bar and Section 1 for context — the account manager owns the numbers; you own the creative.

A. Every-Week Creative Rhythm (Mon → Fri)
MON — PLAN. Pick this week's ONE format focus (see rotation below) and choose the 5 Persona × Stage cells you'll write — lean into the under-exploited early stages (S1–S2) the account is missing. One concept per cell; no two cells should cluster.
TUE — WRITE. Draft copy for each of the 5 cells in natural voice — headline + primary text per concept. Add the dramatization disclaimer to anything where a person "speaks." Keep one idea per ad.
WED — MAKE. Build the 5 creatives in this week's format. Deliberately vary multiple diversity signals — talent, setting, angle, format — so the five concepts read as distinct assets, not one asset five times. Export Gmail/Reddit/Twitter statics in both dark + light.
THU — PACKAGE. Assemble each finished concept for hand-off: headline + primary text + creative file, labeled by Persona · Stage · Angle so it's traceable. Hand the packaged set to whoever runs the account.
FRI — REVIEW + RELOAD. Look at which angles / hooks / formats are resonating — organic signals (saves, shares, comments, replies) plus whatever the account manager reports back. Turn winners into fresh variations for next week, retire stale ones, and refill the queue from the Angle Bank.
One concept per cell: five cells, five distinct concepts. If two concepts only swap a sender name or a single word, they'll read as the same asset — collapse them and pull a genuinely different cell or angle instead.
B. Packaging each concept for hand-off:
  • Headline. The on-image / on-screen line.
  • Primary text. The natural-voice body copy, disclaimer included where a person speaks.
  • Creative file. The finished asset — statics shipped dark + light.
  • Label. Tag every concept by Persona · Stage · Angle so it's traceable at a glance and the account manager can slot it wherever it belongs.

D. How to Refill the Machine
  • Winners → fresh variations. When an angle, hook or format is resonating — organic signals (saves, shares, comments, replies) plus what the account manager reports back — spin 2–3 fresh variations for next week: new sender/name, new hook line, dark↔light, or the same angle shifted one stage (e.g. take a winning S3 into S2 to widen the top of funnel).
  • Stale → retire and pull a new Angle-Bank angle. When a concept goes stale, don't rebuild the same miss. Pull a fresh angle from the Angle Bank (Section 7) — especially net-new S1–S2 and meme angles — and write it into the same Persona × Stage cell with a new format.
  • Fill the matrix over ~3 months. Work the 25-cell Persona × Awareness matrix systematically: each week closes a few empty cells. Priority order = under-exploited early stages (S1/S2) first, then round out mid/late. In ~12 weeks every persona has early, mid, and late concepts written and packaged.
E. If you only do one thing this week

Ship 3 new concepts in this week's format across 3 different stages — including at least one early-stage (S1/S2) concept — packaged with headline + primary text + creative file and labeled by Persona · Stage · Angle, ready to hand off.

8. Compliance & Brand-Voice Notes

Legal / Meta guardrails (non-negotiable):
  • No outcome or settlement guarantees. Always conditional: "could be worth," "may qualify," "you could be owed." Never "you will get X" or "guaranteed."
  • Testimonials = dramatizations. Any ad where a person appears to describe a result must carry: "Dramatization based on a real case. Past results do not guarantee a similar outcome."
  • No attorney-client relationship implied. Mirror the LP: submitting the quiz does not create an attorney-client relationship.
  • Attorney advertising. Treat as attorney advertising; keep "The CEO Lawyer / Ali Awad" attribution consistent and honor state-licensing / approved-states limits ("Approved States" in ad names).
  • No specific $ promise tied to the viewer. The "$20k–$250k average" and "$675k for Jose" are framed as ranges / a specific past client — never as what this viewer will receive.
  • Special-Ad-Category discipline. Avoid personal-attribute targeting; let creative do the targeting (broad + persona-led).
Brand voice (Copy Codex applied):
  • Sound like a person who's been through it, not a law firm. First-person, plain, a little blunt.
  • One idea per ad: don't get lowballed. Isolate the strongest line on its own ("It wasn't." / "You're not.").
  • Data creates the drama — real numbers ($675k, "45 seconds," "6 questions") in complete sentences, not staccato bursts.
  • Pair every reframe with emotional absolution ("You've been through enough already," "It's not on you to fight alone").
  • Kill AI-tells (unlock, leverage, seamless, supercharge). No announcement phrases ("Here's the truth…").
  • Spanish copy in natural US-Spanish, not translated-English. Keep the Sanchez proof front-and-center for P4.
