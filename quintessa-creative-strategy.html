Vehicle Injury Claim — MVA Lead-Gen

Offer: Free 60-second "do I qualify" survey that tells motor-vehicle-accident victims if they may be owed a payout — The Complete Compensation Program™. No Win, No Fee. Leads routed to partner attorneys.

Funnel: Meta paid → GHL quiz LP (vehicleinjuryclaim.com/get-started-a) → phone/email capture → attorney intake. Geo: Arizona-first.

1 · Offer Snapshot

What / Price: Free, no-obligation eligibility survey. Zero cost to the lead; attorney fees are contingency ("No Win, No Fee"). The user's "product" is the answer to "Is my accident worth pursuing?"

Audience: Arizona residents (primary) who were in a motor vehicle accident — as driver or passenger — where they were injured and were not primarily at fault. Windows accepted from within 3 days out to 2+ years post-accident.

Landing page: vehicleinjuryclaim.com/get-started-a — dark crash-scene hero, blue "Almost Complete" progress bar, quiz: injury Y/N → accident timing → fault / medical treatment / current representation → contact capture.

Proof / trust pillars (from LP):

  • No Win, No Fee — pay nothing unless the case wins
  • Maximum Compensation — "every dollar you're entitled to"
  • Expert Guidance — team handles paperwork + negotiation
  • Proven Track Record — "helped countless accident victims"
  • Client-First — communication, transparency, results

Formats in use (mined from assets): article/advertorial statics, Gmail- & inbox-style statics, testimonial portraits (e.g. Nikki Reaves), AI-generated legal lifestyle imagery. Under-used: Reels/UGC talking-head, meme, split-screen.

5 · Week-by-Week Execution Playbook (Dummy-Proof)

For a junior buyer or VA running this account. Follow it literally. Everything targets Arizona geo, Meta placements, driving to vehicleinjuryclaim.com/get-started-a. The job each week: build new ads, launch them, read the numbers, kill losers, feed winners.

A · Every-Week Operating Rhythm

MonBUILD. Pull this week's format focus (§6 calendar) + the 5 matrix cells. Write/brief 5 new ads in that format. Confirm each cell varies talent + setting + angle (beat Andromeda similarity).
TueLAUNCH. Load all 5 new ads into C1 (Testing) — each concept in its OWN new ad set, AZ geo, broad/advantage+ audience. Check pixel fires on quiz-start. Confirm disclaimers on every creative.
WedLEARN. Do not touch. Let ad sets exit learning. Only fix broken links / disapprovals. Note any early quiz-start signal but take no action.
ThuREAD. Pull spend + cost/qualified lead per ad against the Kill/Keep/Scale table (§B). Flag winners, losers, and "on the fence."
FriCUT + FEED. Kill losers. Move winners' post IDs into C2 (Scaling). Queue 2–3 spins of each winner for next Monday's build. Log everything in the tracker.

Weekly testing budget: run each of the 5 new C1 ad sets at roughly a fixed daily test budget so every concept can reach the spend gate in the table below within ~3–4 days. If unsure, size the daily budget at 1–2× your target cost-per-qualified-lead per ad set so a losing ad can spend to the gate (≈2× CPL) inside the week without overspending. Keep C1 total steady week to week; scale extra budget only inside C2.

The 2-Campaign Method:

  • C1 — Testing: where ALL new ads are born. One new ad = one new ad set. Never edit a live winner here; you test fresh creative only.
  • C2 — Scaling: holds only winning post IDs (use existing post so it keeps its social proof / comments). Increase budget here. Nothing unproven enters C2.

Ad naming convention (use every time):

QUIN_[Persona P1–P5]_[Stage S1–S5]_[Format]_[Angle]_[Talent/Setting]_v[##] e.g. QUIN_P4_S4_UGC_Passenger_FemPax-CarInt_v01

B · Kill / Keep / Scale Rules

Primary metric = cost per qualified lead (secondary: cost per booked/attorney-accepted qualified lead). Judge only after the spend gate. Win threshold for a concept = it produces 3–10 qualified leads at or under target CPL.

D · How to Refill the Machine

  • Winners → promote the post ID into C2, then brief 2–3 spins of it for next week's C1 (new hook, new headline, new talent/setting — same proven angle). A winner should never be alone.
  • Losers → don't just delete the idea. Pull the underlying angle back into the §7 Angle Bank and rebuild it in a different format/stage before retiring it. Most "dead" angles die from execution, not the insight.
  • Fill the matrix over ~3 months: each week clears a few cells. Rotate personas and stages so all 25 cells (5 personas × 5 stages) get tested, refreshing winners and quietly retiring what never converts.

If you only do one thing this week: launch 5 new advertorial-static ads (one per persona, ≥2 in Problem-Aware/Unaware) into C1 at AZ geo, let them hit the spend gate, then move any concept that produces 3+ qualified leads into C2.

8 · Compliance & Brand-Voice Notes

Legal lead-gen guardrails — every creative must obey:

  • No settlement or outcome guarantees. Never state or imply a specific dollar amount you will get, or that they will win. "MAJOR payout" language stays on the LP; ad copy uses conditional framing.
  • Conditional "may qualify" language only. Use "may," "could," "see if you qualify" — never "you qualify" or "you are owed."
  • Not legal advice. Ads are informational; include "not legal advice / consult a licensed attorney" where a claim, deadline, or offer is discussed.
  • Testimonials require disclaimers. Real clients only, with signed release: tag "Actual client. Compensated. Individual results vary." If a paid actor, disclose "Dramatization / paid actor."
  • No Win, No Fee accuracy. Clarify it refers to attorney fees; note other case costs may apply. Don't imply "free" means zero cost in every scenario.
  • Deadline claims stay general. Reference that time limits exist; don't cite a specific statute period as advice for the viewer's case.
  • No implying a known personal condition. Don't assert the viewer is injured or has a case; invite them to check.
  • Meta policy: avoid sensational accident gore; dark crash imagery OK but tasteful. This vertical is NOT a Special Ad Category, but personal-attribute targeting rules and personal-health-implication rules still apply — write to the situation, not "you."

Brand voice: Empathetic, plain-spoken, "friend who's been through it." Reassuring, never ambulance-chasing. Copy Codex: sound spoken not written, one idea per paragraph, isolate the strongest claim on its own line, pair the logical reframe ("passengers aren't at fault") with emotional absolution ("you don't have to blame anyone"). Kill AI-tells (unlock, seamless, leverage, game-changer). Default geo personalization to Arizona.
