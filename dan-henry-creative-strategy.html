2 · Micro-Personas

P1 — The Call-Slave Coach

Primary desire: get off the phone and sell while they sleep.
Core fear: that their income is permanently chained to how many calls they (or a closer) can grind out this week.
Core belief: "High-ticket only closes on a call." (This is the belief we break.)
Angle it unlocks: the no-sales-calls webinar — one presentation replaces the whole calendar. Chlamydia angle lives here.

P2 — The Sales-Team Hostage

Primary desire: own their revenue instead of renting it from commission-hungry closers.
Core fear: the team walks, poaches clients, or slacks off — and the business stalls overnight.
Core belief: "You need a sales team to scale high-ticket." (False — Dan did $40M with none.)
Angle it unlocks: "Fire the sales-team bros" — master presentation selling and you don't need them.

P3 — The Webinar Ghost-Towner

Primary desire: a webinar that actually converts instead of a room that watches and vanishes.
Core fear: they've already tried webinars, got a 1% close, and decided "webinars are dead."
Core belief: "My offer/price/traffic is the problem." (No — it's the script structure.)
Angle it unlocks: same offer, same price, same traffic — one script change generated $9.2M. It was never your offer.

P4 — The Course-Launch Burnout

Primary desire: predictable sales without another 10-day launch that eats their life.
Core fear: that they'll keep trading a huge adrenaline sprint for a small, unreliable payday.
Core belief: "Selling means launches, funnels, and endless email sequences."
Angle it unlocks: one evergreen presentation you run on repeat vs. the launch treadmill.

P5 — The Skeptical Scroller (Guru-Fatigued)

Primary desire: a real method with receipts, not another "6-figure" screenshot from a 22-year-old.
Core fear: getting burned again by a $97 that turns out to be a 90-minute pitch for a $10K program.
Core belief: "Everyone teaching webinars is broke and selling the dream."
Angle it unlocks: contrarian / receipts-first — 28x ROAS on a real client's first webinar, $495 in, $14K out, and a refund if it doesn't work.

5 · Week-by-Week Execution Playbook (Dummy-Proof)

Follow this like a recipe. The whole machine has one job: spend cheap on the $97 MDW ticket to acquire buyers, then let retargeting turn those buyers into booked Done-For-You Webinar applications (the real money). The front-end ticket doesn't need to be hugely profitable — it needs to pay for itself while filling the pipeline. Every earnings figure used in copy stays compliant: real-but-exceptional, "results not typical / individual results vary," and never a guarantee.

A · Every-Week Operating Rhythm (Mon → Fri)
DayDo thisExactly what it means
Mon — BUILDWrite & brief the week's conceptsPick the week's ONE focus format + 5 matrix cells (see calendar below). Write copy in Dan's voice, add the income disclaimer to every claim, send briefs to editors/designers. Name every ad (convention below).
Tue — LAUNCHLoad into C1 (testing) & publishDrop the finished ads into Campaign 1 (testing). One ad set, all new ads inside it, so Meta compares them fairly. Turn on. Confirm the CTA link is getclients.com/mdw-a.
Wed — LEARNLeave it aloneDo NOT touch budgets or turn ads off today. The algorithm needs a day to spread spend. Just confirm everything is delivering and spending.
Thu — READCheck the numbers against the gateOpen Ads Manager. For each ad look at spend, cost per MDW ticket, front-end ROAS, and (from the back end) any booked DFY applications. Compare to the Kill/Keep/Scale table.
Fri — CUT & SCALEKill losers, scale winnersTurn off ads that hit the kill rule. Duplicate winning post IDs into Campaign 2 (scaling) and raise budget per the SCALE rule. Note what won so Monday's build copies that pattern.
  • Weekly testing budget: a simple rule of thumb is ~$20–$50/day per new ad in C1, and don't judge any single ad until it has spent roughly 1–2x the target cost per MDW ticket (enough for a fair read). If total budget is tight, run fewer ads at full budget rather than many ads starved of spend.
  • The 2-Campaign Method: C1 = Testing (new creative every week, small budgets, the audition room). C2 = Scaling (only proven winning post IDs get duplicated in here, bigger budget, the money room). Winners graduate from C1 → C2; C2 keeps the same post ID so it inherits the social proof/comments it already earned.
  • Ad naming convention: Persona_Stage_Format_Framework_Hook_vXX — e.g. P1_S2_CreatorVid_PAS_Confession_v01. Same base name, bump the version (v02, v03...) for each spin. This is how you tell at a glance what's winning without opening every ad.
B · Kill / Keep / Scale Rules
DecisionTriggerWhat you do
Spend gateAd has spent < ~1–2x target cost per MDW ticketDo nothing yet. Not enough data. Let it keep running.
KILLPast the gate AND cost per MDW ticket is too high (well above target / front-end ROAS underwater) AND zero booked DFY applicationsTurn it off. It's not funding the front end and it's not filling the pipeline. Send its angle back to the Angle Bank for a rebuild.
KEEP / iteratePast the gate, cost per ticket near target OR at least one application trickling in, but not yet validatedLeave it on and spin 2–3 variations (new hook, new opener, new visual) to push it over the line. Bump the version number.
SCALEValidated = 3–5 front-end sales OR 3–10 booked DFY applications at an acceptable cost per ticketDuplicate the winning post ID into C2 and raise budget ~20–30% every 2–3 days as long as cost per ticket + application flow hold. Never double overnight — that resets learning.

Validated threshold (say it out loud before you call anything a win or loss): 3–5 front-end sales or 3–10 booked DFY applications per concept. Below that you don't have a verdict — you have noise.

D · How to Refill the Machine (keep it running for ~3 months)
  • Winners → push the post ID into C2, then immediately produce 2–3 spins of it (same core insight, new hook/opener/talent) so you have a fresh batch riding the proven angle before it fatigues.
  • Losers → don't mourn them. Pull the angle from §7 Angle Bank, rebuild it in a different format/stage, and relaunch in C1. A dead video angle often wins as a static and vice-versa.
  • Fill the matrix over ~3 months: the goal is to eventually touch every Persona × Stage cell, prioritizing the under-served early stages. Each week you retire dead cells and light up new ones, so the matrix is always ~80% covered with live, learning creative.
If you only do one thing this week: launch 3–5 NEW ads into C1 aimed at an early-stage (S1/S2) cell nobody else is running, let them spend past the gate, and duplicate any winner into C2. Everything else is optional.

8 · Compliance & Brand-Voice Notes

Income-claim guardrails (coaching / info-product)
  • Every earnings figure ($9.2M, $14K/28x, $40M) must be framed as a real but exceptional result and carry "results not typical / individual results vary."
  • No guaranteed-income promises. Never imply the buyer "will" make X. Use "can," "a client did," and attribute to offer + effort.
  • The 30-day money-back guarantee is a refund promise, not an earnings promise — keep those separate.
  • Avoid "get rich," "financial freedom guaranteed," or implying quitting a job. Keep claims tied to a documented case with disclaimer.
  • No fake scarcity beyond the genuine live-seat cap; don't invent countdown lies.
Brand voice — Dan Henry
  • Crass, polarizing, uncomfortable copy outperforms — pick a villain (calendar, sales-team bros, launch model, fake gurus) and don't hedge.
  • Keep the edge, but every polarizing claim rides on a concrete proof point and a disclaimer — that's what keeps it compliant AND credible.
  • Sound spoken, not written: no three-word staccato, no "Here's the truth…" announcements, one idea per paragraph, isolate the strongest claim on its own line.
  • Kill AI-tells: delve, leverage, unlock, seamless, supercharge, game-changer.
  • Pair the reframe with emotional absolution: "It was never your offer."
  • CTA routing: cold → $97 MDW ticket (getclients.com/mdw-a); retargeting → DFY Webinar application.
