2 · Micro-Personas (psychographic, not demographic)

P1 — The Burned Shopper

Primary desire: a price that won't change on move day  •  Core fear: getting quoted low then ambushed with fuel/stair/long-carry fees once the truck is loaded  •  Core belief: "every mover lowballs the quote and pads the bill."

Unlocks: Cost Exposure + binding-quote transparency. This is the account's richest emotional vein — it powers Social Proof Shock (the #1 angle) and Cost Exposure (emerging winner) at once.

P2 — The Overwhelmed Family Mover

Primary desire: to hand the whole move to someone competent and stop juggling it  •  Core fear: the move swallowing their family for weeks — packing at midnight, renting a truck, breaking things  •  Core belief: "a cross-country move is going to be chaos no matter what."

Unlocks: Emotional Relief + Full-Service ("you don't lift a finger"). Softer, warmer register — pairs with UGC and AppleNote. Under-served vs. the price-heavy library.

P3 — The Deal Validator

Primary desire: proof they got the best price before they commit  •  Core fear: signing with the wrong company and finding out a neighbor paid half  •  Core belief: "there's a better deal out there if I just compare one more."

Unlocks: Savings Quantification with concrete math ($4,200 vs $1,597) + "get one more quote before you sign." Most Aware / bottom-funnel closer.

P4 — The Quiet Planner

Primary desire: to get ahead of a move they know is coming in a few months  •  Core fear: leaving it too late and paying a rush premium  •  Core belief: "I have time, I'll deal with it later" — hasn't questioned pricing yet.

Unlocks: the under-exploited early stages. Curiosity/Secret hooks ("the hidden fee in 90% of moving quotes") pull this cold audience in before they've gathered a single quote — key to the colder-audience mandate.

VAM's proven formats map cleanly onto the 5-format system. Faceless "screenshot" statics are the workhorse (Ugly-Ad logic — native, unbranded, text-forward); UGC video is the reach engine. Meme and pure lifestyle are calculated risks here.

7 · Compliance & Brand-Voice Notes

Broker, not carrierVAM is a licensed household-goods broker (DOT #3488475, MC #1147286) coordinating a vetted carrier network — not the truck owner. Don't state "our trucks / our drivers" as literal fact. Safe: "full-service, licensed & insured nationwide," "a dedicated crew handles your move." Truck photos are fine as brand imagery.
No flat-price guaranteeAlways "starting at $1,597" or a real per-customer example ("her quote came back at $1,890"). Price is weight × distance + options. Never promise a fixed total sight-unseen.
"Binding" carefully"Transparent, binding quote / no surprise fees" is the on-brand promise and matches the site. Don't extend it to "guaranteed no additional cost ever" — added services or inventory changes can move the price. Frame as "the quote you get is the price you pay for the move you described."
No delivery-date guaranteeInterstate delivery windows vary. Avoid "delivered by X" / "guaranteed on-time." Calendar/scheduling messaging is also a confirmed performance loser — double reason to skip it.
Proof numbersVerify "12,000+ families" and "4.8★" against source before scaling — public Trustpilot shows ~4.3★ "Excellent." If unconfirmed, soften to "thousands of families" / "rated Excellent on Trustpilot."
No geo in creativeCity-to-city and NY-specific copy are confirmed losers and narrow the audience. Keep creative nationwide; let the algorithm place it.
Brand voiceStatic: direct, confident, savings-focused — a friend who found a deal and is sharing it. Video: authentic, relieved, genuinely surprised. No corporate polish, no announcement phrases, no AI-tells (unlock, seamless, game-changer).
