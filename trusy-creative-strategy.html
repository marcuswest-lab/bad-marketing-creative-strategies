Week-by-Week Execution Playbook (Dummy-Proof)

Zero-guesswork operating system for running Trusy's Meta account. Follow the rhythm, apply the kill/keep/scale rules, work the 4-week calendar, then refill the machine. Everything is judged on new subscriptions (trial→paid, CAC vs. LTV), not surface metrics.

A) Every-Week Operating Rhythm

Same five days, every week. Build → launch → learn → read → cut.

  • Mon — BUILD: Pull last week's numbers. Pick the week's one format focus (see calendar) and the 5 matrix cells. Write/spec 5 new ads. Duplicate winners for scaling.
  • Tue — LAUNCH: Load the 5 new ads into C1 (Testing) as separate ad sets (1 ad each) or a single ABO ad set — one creative per ad so you can read each cleanly. Confirm tracking fires to the trusy.co checkout (trial start + paid).
  • Wed — LEARN: Let ads spend. Do not touch. Check delivery/approval only (watch for compliance disapprovals on fake-follower wording).
  • Thu — READ: First real read at the spend gate. Tag each ad KILL / KEEP / SCALE per the rules table. Note which are approaching the 3–5 subscriber validation line (Tier 1).
  • Fri — CUT & SCALE: Kill losers. Move validated post IDs into C2 (Scaling). Log CAC vs. subscription value + early churn/refund signals (10-day guarantee window matters). Queue next week's build.
Weekly testing budget: Fund C1 so each of the 5 test ads can reach the spend gate in ~3 days — roughly 1–1.5× a single plan price per ad ($100–$150/ad ≈ $500–$750/wk to start). Scale total budget up only as C2 winners prove CAC < LTV. Never starve a test below the gate or you're reading noise.
Exact 2-Campaign Method:
C1 — Testing: all net-new creative. Low/controlled budget, broad-ish targeting, 1 creative per ad. Job = find winners.
C2 — Scaling: only validated winning post IDs (use the same post ID so social proof carries). Higher budget, CBO, layer lookalikes/broad. Job = spend behind proven winners.
Naming convention: Trusy_C1_[Format]_[Persona]_[Stage]_[Angle]_[MMDD]_v# — e.g. Trusy_C1_UglyAd_BurnedBuyer_S2_FakeFollowerSabotage_0713_v1. Scaling copies keep the same tail with C2.

Self-serve note: checkout is self-serve subscription — judge a creative on trial→paid conversion and CAC vs. LTV, not clicks or trial starts alone. Watch churn and 10-day refunds; a creative that pulls cheap trials that all cancel is a loser, not a winner.

D) How to Refill the Machine

  • Winners → C2 + 2–3 spins: every validated ad gets duplicated into scaling and reproduced as 2–3 variants (new hook line, new visual, same winning insight) fed back into C1. Winning insights don't retire — they get milked across formats/personas.
  • Losers → Angle-Bank rebuild: a killed hook isn't a dead angle. Send it to the Angle Bank, rebuild it for a different persona/stage or format, and re-enter it later. Kill the execution, not the idea.
  • Fill the matrix over ~3 months: 5 cells/week × ~12 weeks systematically covers every Persona × Stage cell, with extra weight on the under-exploited S1/S2 education plays. The matrix is the map; the calendar walks it.
  • Replenish supply: when the Angle Bank's "net-new" list runs low, mine fresh angles from new winners, comments, reviews, and competitor ads so C1 never runs dry.
If you only do one thing this week: launch the 5 test ads in C1 on Tuesday, read them at the spend gate on Thursday, and on Friday move any ad with 3+ paid subscribers into C2 — that single loop is 80% of the results.

Compliance & Brand-Voice Notes

  • Platform TOS / social-growth risk (biggest one): Meta and Instagram are the same company — do not use language that reads as "buy followers," "grow followers automatically," or anything implying automation/bots against IG's terms. Trusy's real edge is organic/authentic growth — keep every claim on the "real, targeted, no bots, no ban risk" side. This also keeps the ad from being disapproved.
  • Outcome / results claims: "up to 300% faster," "400–600 followers/mo," and "2,500+ guaranteed" come from the LP tiers — attribute results to typical/plan-level outcomes, avoid stating a specific viewer will hit a number. Pair strong numbers with the 10-day money-back guarantee rather than a personal promise.
  • No personal-attribute call-outs: don't imply Meta knows the viewer's account is failing ("your account is dying"). Frame the problem as the algorithm/market, not "you specifically." Softer identity call-outs ("if you own a business…") are fine.
  • Guarantee language: only state the 10-day risk-free/refund exactly as it appears on the LP; don't invent lifetime or unconditional guarantees.
  • Brand voice: cool, quirky, creator-friend tone. Sounds spoken, not written. One idea per short paragraph, isolate the strongest claim on its own line, pair every reframe with absolution ("it was never your fault"). Kill AI-tells (unlock, leverage, seamless, supercharge, game-changer).
