1 · Offer Snapshot

WhatA free, no-obligation case-value / eligibility assessment. The prospect answers a short set of questions about their accident (type, timing, state, treatment, fault, current attorney status) and, in ~60 seconds, gets an estimate of whether their claim may be worth more than the insurance offer. Qualified leads are routed to an intake team, then to a partner attorney.
Price / riskFree to check. No upfront cost to the client — attorney representation runs on contingency. The ad-side promise is the assessment, never a settlement amount.
Core mechanism"The first insurance offer is almost never what the case is worth." The form surfaces categories most victims leave out (ongoing medical, lost income, future care) so they can see the gap before they sign.
AudienceRecent U.S. accident victims (ideally within 30 days, statute window up to 24 months), roughly 35–44, lower-to-middle income, injured, financially pressured, sitting on or expecting a settlement offer. Broad U.S. targeting, later optimized by state.
Landing pagego.topcrashlawyer.com/landing-page — headline "Did Insurance Pay You Enough After Your Accident?" 11-step form. Page carries the "Attorney Advertising" label and the Meta non-affiliation notice.
Proven proof assetsNative testimonial screenshots (Reddit r/LifeProTips, Twitter/X, Gmail) built as dramatizations; settlement-gap framing ($X almost accepted → $Y+ actual worth); qualification checklists; before/after split visuals; AI-UGC talking-head videos.
Formats live nowUgly/text-on-image statics (Payout Disparity, Pain Cost Spiral), Qualification checklists, native social-screenshot statics (Reddit/Twitter/Gmail), AI-UGC Reels.
What's workingPremature Settlement Risk (native testimonial screenshots) is the clear volume winner — ~$51K spend, 34 signed cases, ~$1,507 CAC. Qualification Check wins on efficiency (~$67 cost/MQL). Offer vs Evaluation and Payout Disparity also flagged winners. AI-UGC video hooks are strong (Breaking News 78% hook rate) but haven't converted to cases yet.

2 · Micro-Personas

A · "The Suspicious Settler"

Primary desireGet every dollar their injury is actually worth — not the fast check.
Core fearSigning away tens of thousands because they didn't know any better.
Core belief"Something about this offer feels off, but I don't know how to prove it."
Unlocks: the payout-gap / "same injury, different check" angle. Validate the gut feeling, then hand them a free way to confirm it before they settle.

B · "The Overwhelmed & Broke"

Primary desireMake the medical bills and lost income stop stacking up.
Core fearTaking a lowball just to survive this month, then drowning anyway.
Core belief"I can't afford to wait, and I can't afford a lawyer either."
Unlocks: the Pain Cost Spiral angle — bills rising while the offer stays flat. Lead with "free, no lawyer needed, 60 seconds" to remove cost + friction fear.

C · "The Lawyer-Averse Skeptic"

Primary desireHandle it without getting dragged into a courtroom or a shark's office.
Core fearThat "getting a lawyer" means stress, fees, and being taken advantage of twice.
Core belief"Lawyers are a hassle; I'd rather just deal with insurance myself."
Unlocks: the "no lawyer, no commitment, just check" angle + native testimonial screenshots (peer, not law firm). Lowers the intimidation wall the funnel exists to solve.

D · "The Rushed Target"

Primary desireGet the insurance adjuster off their back with confidence, not panic.
Core fearBeing pressured into a bad decision while still hurt and foggy.
Core belief"They keep calling — maybe I should just take it and be done."
Unlocks: "the faster they push, the more it's worth" — name the insurer as the antagonist and reframe fast offers as a tell. Adds urgency without an outcome promise.

E · "The Wait-and-See Under-Treater"

Primary desireBelieve it "wasn't that bad" and move on with life.
Core fear(Latent) — hasn't yet realized a soft-tissue injury can be a real claim.
Core belief"It was a minor accident. I'm a little sore. There's nothing to claim."
Unlocks: the Unaware / education angle — "minor" whiplash and soreness that turns into a real case. This is the under-worked top of funnel (see matrix).

5 · Week-by-Week Execution Playbook (Dummy-Proof)

Follow this literally. Nothing here promises a settlement outcome — every ad still sells the free case check, never a dollar result. Primary KPI is cost/MQL < $250; a creative is validated when its leads produce signed / pending-signed cases at ≈ $1,500 cost/SQL and CAC ≤ $1,800. Batch-level win = 3–5 signed cases.

Budget: run ~$1,500–$2,500/week of testing spend in Campaign 1, split so each new creative gets a fair read (aim ~$250–$400 behind each ad before you judge it — see Kill/Keep/Scale). Winners move to Campaign 2 and get the scaling budget.

D · How to Refill the Machine
  • Winners → duplicate the post ID into Campaign 2, then immediately spin 2–3 variations of it — new hook, new talent, or ported to a new format — and queue them into next week's Campaign 1 test.
  • Losers → don't just retry the same idea. Pull a fresh line from the Angle Bank (§7) — e.g. "First offer = opening bid," "A form, not a firm," the adjuster-call script, or the "why the offer comes so fast" curiosity reframe — and rebuild the cell around it.
  • Coverage goal → each week fill more of the Persona × Awareness matrix. Over ~3 months (12+ batches) push toward full-matrix coverage, keeping steady pressure on the Unaware column so top-of-funnel finally converts to signed cases, not just cheap hooks.
If you only do one thing this week: launch this week's 5 new ads into Campaign 1, leave them alone to learn, and on Friday duplicate any ad under $250 cost/MQL (with real intake interest) into Campaign 2.

8 · Compliance & Brand-Voice Notes

GuardrailRule for every Case Source ad
No outcome guaranteesNever promise or guarantee a settlement amount. Frame all numbers as illustrative and conditional ("cases can be worth," "may qualify," "depending on the details"). The ad promises the free check, not the money.
Dramatizations must be labeledAll testimonial screenshots (Reddit/Twitter/Gmail) are dramatizations using stock photos, not real clients. Every asset carries the disclaimer legibly on-image.
Required disclaimer"Attorney Ad. Each case is unique; past results do not guarantee future outcomes. This message includes a dramatization using a stock photo and is not a real client or real event. Legal outcomes vary based on the specific facts, evidence, and applicable law. Completing a case review form does not create an attorney-client relationship."
No personal legal attributesAvoid implying "you have a case," "you are owed $X," or "you will win." Keep it conditional: "you may qualify," "see if your accident qualifies." Don't imply a viewer's specific medical or legal condition.
Meta non-affiliationThe firm is not affiliated with or endorsed by Meta/Facebook/Instagram (stated on LP; don't contradict in ad copy).
No attorney-client relationshipFilling the form is a case review, not representation. Never imply the prospect is "now a client."
Text coverageKeep on-image text under ~20% for delivery; ensure legibility at 400px width (mobile-first).
Brand voiceDirect, authoritative, and empathetic to financial stress without being patronizing. A trusted advisor pre-screening you — like a bank pre-qualifying a loan — not a pushy TV lawyer. No legal jargon, no hype, no fear-mongering beyond the honest stakes.
